Overview

I attend Valentines High School in Ilford where I currently take four A levels of Maths, Further Maths, Physics and Chemistry.

Due to my choice of subjects, I have minimal coursework as most of my subjects are entirely exam based such as Maths.

Mathematics

The Mathematics course I am currently taking involves three modules this year and a further three modules next year.
  • - Core 1
  • - Core 2
  • - Core 3
  • - Core 4
  • - Mechanics 1

As well as one undecided module that will be chosen next year. As Mathematics is 100% exam based, there is no coursework; although teachers regularly set exam papers to do outside of school (at least one a week), they are merely for preperation for the final exam and don't count towards our grades at all.

Further Mathematics

Further Maths is almost identical to the Maths course aside for the modules taken.

  • - Further Pure 1
  • - Decision 1
  • - Statistics 1
  • - Further Pure 2

And an additional 2 more modules to be taken next year.

This subject is also entirely exam based so aside from doing regular exam practice papers - there is no coursework. By clicking the buttons below; past papers from previous years can be found.

Physics

Physics is a subject that has two seperate grading systems, one for the exam and another for the practical skills assessment.

There are three physics exam at the end of Year 13, two longer papers and one shorter paper, these add up to 100% of your final grade.

There is a practical skills assessment that does not have any weighting on your grade as it is entirely pass or fail.

There are six modules total; Practical skills, Foundations, Forces and Motion, Electrons, Waves and Photons, Newtonian World and Astrophysics, Medical Physics.

Chemistry

Chemistry like physics also has a series of exams and a practical skills asssessment

There are two exams at the end of Year 13 which test the three modules, Organic Chemistry, Inorganic Chemistry and Physical Chemistry. Each exam is worth 50% of the grade

Across the two years of the course; there are several practicals that come up that must be conducted correctly and then written up to see if the student has understood all the safety precautions. This is a pass or fail skills assessment

The key practicals I have currently (as of 27/03/17) already completed are Rates of Reaction and Titrations. Both practicals written up in the Chemistry Exam Book.

Goals

After taking Chemistry for AS and taking Maths, Further Maths and Physics for A-Levels; I intend to apply to either Cambridge or Oxford for Computer Science in order to expand my knowledge on the subject I find most interesting.
